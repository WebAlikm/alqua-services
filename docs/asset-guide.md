# Visual Asset Guide

The interface uses generated prototype assets in stable, responsive containers. Replace the community concept image with an approved local photograph when one becomes available; the dimensions will not require layout changes.

## 1. Al Qua'a Community Photograph

**Current file:** `public/images/al-quaa-community.webp`

The current image is AI-generated and is labelled as a concept in the interface. It must not be presented as documentary evidence or a photograph of identifiable Al Qua'a residents.

**Recommended size:** 1600 x 640 pixels, WebP, under 250 KB.

**Image direction:** A bright, authentic daytime photograph of everyday life in Al Qua'a. The strongest image would show a recognizable local service context such as residents approaching a community facility, a clinic or majlis entrance, or a farm-service interaction. Include people only with permission and avoid identifiable children unless guardian consent is documented.

**Composition:** Keep the main subject near the center or right third. Preserve open space on the opposite side so Arabic and English text remain readable if the image is later used with an overlay.

**Avoid:** Dubai or Abu Dhabi skyline imagery, generic empty desert stock photography, dark cinematic grading, staged technology scenes, or visuals that misrepresent Al Qua'a.

**Suggested alt text:** `Residents accessing local community services in Al Qua'a, Abu Dhabi.`

## 2. Al Qua'a Services Project Logo

**Files:** `public/images/alqua-services-logo.png` and `public/images/alqua-services-logo-master.png`

The optimized 512 x 512 transparent PNG is used by the interface. The 1254 x 1254 transparent master is retained for future exports.

**Logo direction:** A simple, flat mark combining the idea of a location gateway, community connection, or service path. It should remain recognizable at 40 pixels and work beside both `خدمات القوع` and `Al Qua'a Services`.

**Color palette:**

- UAE green: `#00843D`
- UAE red: `#D71920`
- Black: `#111111`
- White: `#FFFFFF`

Do not copy the UAE coat of arms, a ministry identity, police branding, or another official government logo. The project must not imply government endorsement unless written authorization is obtained.

## 3. Partner Logos

Add provider logos only after listings are verified and the provider has approved their use. Prefer a single-color version in the details view rather than a crowded logo strip. Keep every logo inside a 120 x 48 pixel area with its original proportions.

## Pride Statement

`Proud of the UAE · نفخر بالإمارات` is a project statement, not an official certification mark. Keep it typographic unless an authorized national campaign asset is provided.
